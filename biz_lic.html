Financial Enterprises
A financial enterprise is a business or organization that primarily engages in activities related to financial services or financial transactions. This can include a wide range of operations such as:
  • Third party collections
  • Consumer lender loans
  • Debt management
  • Escrow
  • Money transmission
  • Financing insurance premiums
  • Financing the purchase of vehicles
  • Title loans
  • Mortgage loan origination
  • Appraisers and appraisal management companies
In general, a financial enterprise either manages money, provides financial advice, or facilitates financial transactions for individuals, businesses, or governments.
Financial Institutions
A financial institution is an organization that provides financial services to its clients or members, primarily involving the management, investment, transfer, or lending of money. Common types of financial institutions include:
  • Banks (commercial and retail)
  • Credit unions
  • Insurance companies
  • Investment firms
  • Brokerages
  • Mortgage companies
Financial institutions play a key role in the economy by facilitating the flow of capital and credit, safeguarding money, and offering financial products and services like loans, savings accounts, insurance, and investment opportunities.
Insurance Companies
An insurance company is a financial institution that provides risk management services by selling insurance policies to individuals or organizations. In exchange for premium payments, the company agrees to compensate the policyholder for specified losses or damages, such as those caused by accidents, illness, natural disasters, or death. Key functions of an insurance company include:
  • Underwriting: Assessing risk and determining policy terms and premiums
  • Claims management: Evaluating and paying out claims when a covered event occurs
  • Risk pooling: Spreading risk across many policyholders to minimize the financial impact of individual losses
Insurance Professionals
An insurance professional is an individual who works in the insurance industry and specializes in advising, selling, managing, or underwriting insurance policies. They help clients understand risk, choose appropriate coverage, and navigate the claims process. Common roles of insurance professionals include:
  • Insurance agents/brokers – Sell policies and advise clients on coverage options
  • Underwriters – Assess risk and determine policy terms and pricing
  • Claims adjusters – Investigate insurance claims and determine payouts
  • Actuaries – Use statistics and mathematics to assess risk and set premium rates
  • Customer service representatives – Assist clients with policy questions, updates, or claims
  • Insurance professionals may work for insurance companies, agencies, or as independent consultants.
Mortgage Lending
A mortgage lender is a financial institution or individual that provides loans to borrowers for the purchase, refinance, or construction of real estate, typically homes or commercial properties. The lender funds the mortgage and sets the terms, including the interest rate, repayment schedule, and loan duration. Common types of mortgage lenders include:
  • Mortgage Brokers (who connect borrowers with lenders but don't lend directly)
  • Mortgage Bankers
  • Loan Originators
The mortgage lender earns revenue primarily through interest payments and may also sell the loan to investors or the secondary mortgage market after it’s issued.
Real Estate Appraisal & Property Tax Agent
A Real Estate Appraiser & Property Tax Agent is a professional who performs two key functions related to property valuation and taxation:

Real Estate Appraiser

A real estate appraiser is responsible for estimating the market value of real property (land and buildings) for purposes such as:
  • Buying or selling property
  • Mortgage lending
  • Tax assessments
  • Legal disputes
  • Estate planning
Appraisers use methods like comparative market analysis, income approach, or cost approach to determine value. They must follow standards like the Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP).

Property Tax Agent

A property tax agent represents property owners in matters related to property tax assessments, including:
  • Reviewing property tax valuations
  • Filing appeals on behalf of the owner
  • Negotiating with local tax assessors
  • Ensuring clients are taxed fairly and accurately
    • These professionals often work with commercial or high-value residential property owners to minimize tax liability.
